The National Flag of the United States

The national flag of the United States, often referred to as the American flag or the U.S. flag, consists of thirteen horizontal stripes, alternating red and white, with a blue rectangle in the canton bearing fifty small, white, five-pointed stars arranged in nine offset horizontal rows, where rows of six stars alternate with rows of five stars. The 50 stars on the flag represent the 50 U.S. states, and the 13 stripes represent the thirteen British colonies that won independence from Great Britain in the American Revolutionary War.

The Idea Behind "Stars and Stripes"

The idea behind "Stars and Stripes" is to reimagine the American flag as a dynamic and interactive website. Instead of a static representation, the flag transforms into a living, clickable experience where each star and stripe links to a deeper exploration of political, social, or cultural themes. The project highlights the complexities of the flag as a symbol of both unity and division. By clicking on various parts of the flag, users are taken to different pages featuring artists, historical moments, or cultural critiques, engaging with the flag beyond its traditional meaning.
